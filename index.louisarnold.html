Common Questions

How to end Poverty?

poverty requires a multi-faceted approach focusing on economic, social, and structural changes. Key strategies include creating well-paying jobs, ensuring access to quality education and healthcare, providing social safety nets (like cash transfers), improving infrastructure, and promoting gender equality.

What is poverty?

Poverty is a state of deprivation where individuals lack the financial resources, income, and assets to meet basic human needs, including food, safe housing, clothing, and healthcare. It is measured in absolute terms (subsistence level) or relative terms (compared to societal standards) and often results in limited access to opportunities, education, and social inclusion.

How can you make a change in the world?

Making a tangible impact on global poverty involves a combination of direct action, such as volunteering, donating to reputable organizations, and sponsoring children, alongside systemic advocacy like lobbying for policy changes, promoting fair wages, and supporting education. Key strategies also include supporting microfinance, empowering women, and improving access to clean water.
